LUNG CANCER

What is it?

Lung cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the lungs and usually occurs when cells in the lung tissue undergo abnormal changes and grow uncontrollably, forming a mass called a tumor. It is one of the most common types of cancer worldwide and is responsible for a significant number of deaths every year.

Early detection

Early detection of lung cancer is crucial for improving the chances of successful treatment and increasing survival rates. A model for early detection of lung cancer can be helpful in identifying individuals who may be at a higher risk of developing lung cancer and detecting the disease at an earlier stage, when it is more treatable. By enabling earlier detection of lung cancer, a model for early detection can help improve patient outcomes, reduce the need for more invasive and costly treatments, and ultimately save lives.
